# MarketPulse AI - Compliance & Legal Framework

## ⚖️ Overview

MarketPulse AI operates in the heavily regulated financial services sector. This document outlines all compliance requirements, legal disclaimers, and risk mitigation strategies.

---

## 📋 Applicable Regulations

### United States
- **Securities Exchange Act (1934)** - Regulates securities trading
- **Investment Advisers Act (1940)** - Defines "investment advice"
- **Anti-Fraud Provisions (Rule 10b-5)** - Prohibits misleading statements
- **Regulation Fair Disclosure (Reg FD)** - Equal access to material info
- **FINRA Rules** - Self-regulatory organization requirements
- **SEC Guidance on AI/ML** - Emerging AI-specific rules

### European Union
- **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** - Data privacy
- **MiFID II (Markets in Financial Instruments Directive)** - Markets regulation
- **EMIR (European Market Infrastructure Regulation)** - Derivatives trading

### Other Jurisdictions
- **FCA (UK)** - Financial Conduct Authority
- **ASIC (Australia)** - Australian Securities and Investments Commission
- **IIROC (Canada)** - Investment Industry Regulatory Organization

## 🚫 What MarketPulse AI CANNOT Do

MarketPulse AI is **NOT**:
1. A licensed financial advisor
2. A registered investment advisor (RIA)
3. A broker-dealer

5. A guarantee of market outcomes
6. A replacement for professional financial consultation

## 🔒 Privacy Policy - GDPR & CCPA Compliance

### GDPR Compliance

**1. Legal Basis for Data Processing**
```
We process personal data based on:
- Contractual necessity (subscription management)
- Legitimate business interests (analytics, security)
- Your explicit consent (marketing communications)
- Legal obligation (fraud prevention, regulatory compliance)
```

**2. User Rights (GDPR Articles 15-20)**
- Right to access: Users can request all data we hold (within 30 days)
- Right to rectification: Users can correct inaccurate data
- Right to erasure: Users can request deletion ("right to be forgotten")
- Right to restrict processing: Users can limit how we use their data
- Right to data portability: Users can export their data in machine-readable format
- Right to object: Users can opt out of analytics/marketing

**3. Data Retention**
```
Account data:
- Retained while account is active
- Deleted 90 days after account closure
- Exception: Billing records retained 7 years (tax law)

Analytics data:
- Anonymized after 90 days
- Completely deleted after 12 months

Contact:
- Retained until explicitly deleted
- Automatically deleted after 24 months of inactivity
```

**4. Data Processing Agreements (DPA)**
```
We use sub-processors (e.g., AWS, Stripe, Datadog) and maintain 
Data Processing Agreements (DPA) with all sub-processors ensuring:
- GDPR compliance
- Standard contractual clauses (SCCs)
- Incident notification requirements
```

### CCPA Compliance (California)

**California Consumer Rights**:
- Right to know: What data is collected and how it's used
- Right to delete: Request deletion of personal information
- Right to opt-out: Opt out of data sales (we don't sell data)
- Right to non-discrimination: No penalty for exercising rights
- Right to limit use: Limit use to disclosed purposes

**CCPA Implementation**:
```
Users can exercise CCPA rights by:
1. Visiting /privacy/ccpa-rights
2. Submitting signed request to privacy@marketpulse.ai
3. Verifying identity (email verification or CCPA verification service)
4. Receiving response within 45 days
```

- Confirmation bias: Seeking information that confirms your view

**1. SEC (Securities & Exchange Commission)**

- Primary contact: Chief Compliance Officer
- Response tone: Cooperative, transparent, factual
- Key talking points:
  - We are NOT providing investment advice

## 🚨 Incident Response Plan

### Data Breach Protocol
```
1. Detect breach → Immediately stop data loss
2. Investigate → Determine scope, severity, impact
3. Notify (72 hours) → Notify affected users and regulators
4. Document → Full incident report
5. Remediate → Fix underlying cause
6. Review → Process improvement

GDPR Notification:
- Local data protection authority within 72 hours
- Individual users with high-risk breaches
- Public notification if >1000 individuals affected
```

### AI Model Failure Protocol
```
1. Detect anomaly → Model accuracy drops below 80%
2. Pause → Disable affected model
3. Investigate → Root cause analysis
4. Notify → Alert users of limitations
5. Retrain → Improve with fresh data
6. Validate → Rigorous testing before re-launch
```

### Market Manipulation Alert Protocol
```
1. Detect → Unusual patterns in sentiment/usage
2. Investigate → Are users manipulating markets with our insights?
3. Report → If found, report to SEC/FINRA
4. Implement → Rate limiting, user restrictions
5. Coordinate → With legal and compliance team
```
